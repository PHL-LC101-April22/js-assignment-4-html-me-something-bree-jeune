From Paramedic to Programmer

"Nothing brought me greater joy than learning new things and problem solving."

Why I Decided to Change Careers

Recent times have left many healthcare workers feeling as though there is much to be desired. I began working in emergency medical services in 2014 with the hopes of reaching a demographic of people who needed care the most. Even though EMS is a rewarding job, I couldn't help but to think that there are other ways that I can change lives.

Unfortunately, like many other providers in this field, I began to experience physical and mental burnout. Being a paramedic is demanding work. Long hours, low emolument, the bodily demand and increasingly sicker populations tend to lend a hand to high turnover rates.

How It Started

As a kid, I always had a keen interest in technology. I recall times where I would take electronics apart and put them back together just so I could see how they were built and functioned. I also truly loved solving problems of any nature. However, if you asked me back then what I wanted to be when I grew up, without any hesitation, I would say a doctor.

Later in life, when I decided to go to college, I was at an empass. Believe it or not, I had no clue what to major in. The only thing I knew for sure, is that I wanted to help people. Pre-med would have been an obvious choice but I was too scared to declare it. I knew it would be hard work and time consuming so I opted for an easier route.

When the time finally came to make a choice, I decided that I was going to major in psychology. I understood that mental health was still a bit taboo but I knew too many people who had suffered emotionally from the unspoken. Ultimately, I would still be adjacent to the medical field and able to make a difference.

I graduated within four years and then the tough choices came. Apply to medical school, apply to graduate school or enter the workforce? I decided again, I was not quite ready for advanced education so I decided to become an EMT. If I was going to be a doctor, I was going to be the best. Being an EMT, would allow me to gain hands-on experience and learn critical decision making in split-seconds. I spent the next few years progressing my skills and knowledge. At present, I operate as a critical care paramedic. I started this role just as the pandemic began to upsurge. This complicated matters greatly because the population of the sick and injured increased exponentially.

Future Plans

Three years later, the effects are still very palpable. I began to reconsider all the things I have ever been passionate about. In my free time, I had dabbled with programming languages like Python and Ruby. Nothing brought me greater joy than learning new things and problem solving.

Since becoming a LaunchCode student, I have learned to accomplish things I would have never imagined. I feel challenged everyday and motivated to succeed.
